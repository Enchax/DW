F.A.Q

  • Why is air quality so important?

    Air pollution causes 1 in 8 deaths worldwide: It is the single biggest environmental health crisis we face! The number of annual deaths caused by air pollution around the world is now greater than malaria, car accidents and HIV combined.

  • What can we do with air quality data?

    Once citizens are aware and informed of the air quality they are breathing, they can limit their exposure to air pollution.

  • What types of pollutants are being monitored worldwide?

    Six main air pollutants are monitored in most countries: ozone (O3), fine particulate matter (PM2.5), particulate matter (PM10), nitrogen dioxide (NO2), sulfur dioxide (SO2), and carbon monoxide (CO).

  • What are the main differences and similarities between air quality and weather?

    Air quality can dramatically change within a very short amount of time, or within a few kilometers, much more so than weather. This is due to a number of different factors.
